Step 02
Speaker & Coverage Defaults
These defaults are used for new zones. Each zone can override them. Coverage is calculated from the speaker's conical dispersion angle (the "-6dB" beamwidth most manufacturers publish) and the mounting height above the listening plane.
Mounting Type
Dispersion & Listening Plane
Listening plane = the height at which even coverage matters most (ear height seated ≈ 3.5–4 ft, standing ≈ 5.5 ft; for background music in retail/hospitality, 4 ft is a common reference). Mounting height used in the coverage formula = Ceiling Height − Listening Plane Height.
Pattern & Overlap
Overlap margin reduces speaker spacing below the theoretical maximum so coverage circles overlap slightly, avoiding "dead spots" between speakers and smoothing the SPL transition. 10–20% is typical for background/foreground music systems.
